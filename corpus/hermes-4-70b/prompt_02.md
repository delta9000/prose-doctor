Rowena hunched over her desk, her nose almost touching the decaying parchment. Brown curls cascaded down her shoulders, tickling the words she’d penned in her notebook. Her pen danced across the page, her brow furrowed in concentration. Each hieroglyphic symbol danced before her eyes, a secret language she’d worked tirelessly to understand. The dead language had baffled scholars for centuries, but Rowena had cracked the code, at last, three months ago. Since then, she’d been working on translating the ancient text of a long-forgotten civilization.

She traced the curves and lines with her fingers, her brain working to decipher the meaning behind each character. She’d been working on this particular passage for several hours, but she was close to understanding its meaning. Her heart raced as she realized what the hieroglyphs were telling her. They described a cataclysmic event, one that would forever change the world. But as Rowena read on, she felt a chill run down her spine. The text described events that hadn’t happened yet. It was as if the ancient civilization had written about the future.

Rowena felt a sense of unease wash over her. She pushed her chair back from her desk and stood up, pacing the room. Her mind raced as she tried to understand what she was reading. Was this some kind of prophecy? Or was it just a coincidence? She didn’t know, but she knew she had to tell someone.

She walked down the hallway to her colleague’s office, Dr. Thompson. She knocked on the door nervously, her heart pounding in her chest. “Come in,” Dr. Thompson called from behind the door. Rowena entered, closing the door behind her.

“Dr. Thompson, I need to talk to you about something,” Rowena said, her voice shaking. She sat down in the chair in front of his desk, her hands wringing together in her lap. Dr. Thompson looked up from his papers, his eyebrow raised. “What is it, Rowena?”

“I’ve been working on translating a text from the ancient civilization, and… well, it describes events that haven’t happened yet,” Rowena said, her voice barely above a whisper.

Dr. Thompson leaned back in his chair, a skeptical look on his face. “Are you sure about that?” he asked. “You know, the ancients had a habit of writing about events in a way that could be interpreted as omens or prophecies. Maybe you’re reading too much into it.”

Rowena shook her head. “No, I’m sure about this,” she insisted. “It’s too specific. It describes a war, a great famine, and a pandemic… and it says they’ll happen within the next few years.”

Dr. Thompson sighed, running a hand through his graying hair. “Rowena, you’re a brilliant linguist, but sometimes our minds can play tricks on us. We see what we want to see. I think you should take a step back, maybe get some rest. You’ve been working too hard lately.”

Rowena felt a lump form in her throat. She didn’t know what to say. She stood up, nodded, and left Dr. Thompson’s office. As she walked back to her desk, she felt a sense of dread wash over her. What if Dr. Thompson was right? What if she was just seeing what she wanted to see?

But deep down, she knew she wasn’t. The text was too specific, too detailed. She sat back down at her desk and stared at the hieroglyphs, trying to make sense of them. She couldn’t let this go. She had to know the truth.

Over the next few days, Rowena threw herself into her work.
