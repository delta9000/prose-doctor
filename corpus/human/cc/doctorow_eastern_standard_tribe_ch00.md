This is the second novel I&#8217;ve made available as a free download (the first, 
Down and Out in the Magic Kingdom
, was downloaded hundreds of thousands of times and sold like hell). I&#8217;m delighted to do it a second time.

Here&#8217;s the deal: I don&#8217;t believe that there&#8217;s any market-demand for teasers or for &#8220;Digital Rights Management&#8221; technology: none of you woke up this morning and said, &#8220;Damn, I wish there was a way I could get less of the books I enjoy and a way I could do less with them once I have them.&#8221; My goal here is to figure out what people actually 
want
 out of electronically delivered text, and so I&#8217;m giving this novel to you in three open and flexible formats with an invitation:

Convert these files to any &#8220;e-book&#8221; or text format you want, and send them to me, along with a note telling me what reader it&#8217;s intended for and I will add it to this page. The only caveats are:

You have to include the entire text of the novel, including (especially!) the Creative Commons license and metadata

If you are converting to a format that has some kind of use-restriction options (i.e., no-print, no-copy, etc), these must be switched 
off

You can&#8217;t include the cover art. That belongs to my publisher, not me

No duplicates: if there&#8217;s already a file available for the reader you use, don&#8217;t send me another one with your favorite tweaks in it &#8212; I&#8217;m not going to mediate catfights about how big the indents should be in Newton eBook Reader files or whatnot and besides, civilians who want to download a copy of the book shouldn&#8217;t have to puzzle out whether they want the version with the curly-quotes or the foot-and-inch-marks

A note for downloaders: download this book. Enjoy it. If you feel so motivated, 
drop me a note
 and tell me how you used it and what you thought (and forgive me if I don&#8217;t get back to you!). If you want to, go ahead and 
buy a copy
 and I&#8217;ll get my royalty. But there&#8217;s no obligation on you to buy it if you&#8217;ve read &#8212; you&#8217;re not ripping me off &#8212; and forgive me, but I&#8217;m not interested in &#8220;tipjar&#8221; payments &#8212; I&#8217;m not in competition with my publisher here. Thanks for understanding, and I hope you enjoy the book.
