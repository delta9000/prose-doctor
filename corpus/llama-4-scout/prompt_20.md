The sun rises over the small town, casting a warm glow through the windows of the bakery. The sweet scent of freshly baked bread wafts through the air, enticing the sleepy residents to start their day. Emma, the baker, expertly kneads a batch of dough, her hands moving with a practiced ease. She has been up since before dawn, mixing and proofing the yeast, waiting for the moment when the bread would be ready to reveal its secrets.

The bakery, aptly named "Remember When," has a loyal following, and Emma takes pride in her craft. She knows that her bread is special, that it holds the power to unlock memories long buried. It's not just the yeast or the flour that makes it so; it's the love and care that Emma puts into every loaf. She's been doing this for years, and the regulars have come to expect the unexpected.

As she slides the perfectly formed loaves into the oven, the doorbell above the entrance jingles, signaling the arrival of a customer. Emma's eyes flicker up to meet the newcomer's, and her heart skips a beat. She knows that face, or at least, she thinks she does. The woman, with her piercing green eyes and raven-black hair, looks familiar, yet Emma can't quite place her.

The woman smiles and approaches the counter, her movements fluid and confident. Emma's hands continue to work on autopilot, shaping a baguette as she greets the customer. "Welcome to Remember When! What can I get for you today?"

The woman's eyes scan the shelves, taking in the assortment of breads and pastries. "Something with a bit of sweetness, please. I have a craving for something comforting."

Emma nods, her mind racing. She knows this woman, or at least, she thinks she does. The memories are hazy, like a half-remembered dream. She pushes the thoughts aside and focuses on the task at hand. "I have just the thing. My honey and oat bread is popular today. It's still warm from the oven."

As she slices into the loaf, the aroma wafts up, enticing the woman. Emma hands her a generous slice, and the woman takes a bite, closing her eyes in rapture. The sweetness of the honey and the earthiness of the oats mingle on her tongue, and for a moment, Emma forgets about her confusion.

The woman's eyes snap open, and she looks at Emma with a curious expression. "This is incredible," she says, taking another bite. As she chews, her eyes begin to glaze, and her face softens. Emma recognizes the look; it's the same one she sees on her customers every day. The bread is working its magic.

The woman's eyes cloud, and she takes a step back, as if she's being pulled into a different world. Emma watches, mesmerized, as the woman's face changes. The lines around her eyes deepen, and her skin takes on a softer, more vulnerable texture. She looks younger, more carefree.

Suddenly, the woman gasps, and her eyes flash open. She looks around the bakery, taking in the familiar sights and smells. Her gaze lands on Emma, and for a moment, they lock eyes. Emma sees a flicker of recognition, and then the woman's face crumples.

"Oh, God," the woman whispers, her voice trembling. "I remember."

Emma's hands still on the counter, her fingers curled around a crusty baguette. She knows what's happening. The bread is unlocking a memory, one that's been buried deep. The woman's eyes well up with tears, and Emma feels a pang of empathy.

The woman takes a step forward, her voice barely above a whisper. "I remember my grandmother's garden. The smell of fresh earth and blooming flowers. I remember helping her plant seeds and harvest vegetables." The memories pour out of her like a flood, and Emma listens, entranced.

As the woman's memories unfold, Emma's confusion grows. She knows this woman, or at least, she thinks she does. The memories she's hearing are not just any memories; they're pieces of her own past. Emma's mind reels as she tries to make sense of it all.

The woman's eyes lock onto Emma's, and for a moment, they share a secret. Emma sees a glimmer of recognition, a sense of shared history. And then, just as quickly, it's gone. The woman smiles, and her face returns to its usual confident mask.

"I think I need to go," the woman says, her voice steady once more. "Thank you for the bread, Emma. It's been a long time since I've remembered."

As the woman exits the bakery, Emma watches her go, her mind reeling. Who was that woman, and why did her bread unlock a piece of Emma's own past? The questions swirl in her mind as she goes back to work, her hands moving on autopilot.

The sun climbs higher in the sky, casting a warm glow over the bakery. The scent of freshly baked bread continues to waft through the air, enticing the townspeople to come and remember. Emma's hands shape another loaf, her mind still reeling from the encounter. She knows that her bread has a way of unlocking memories, but she never expected it to unlock a piece of her own past.

As the day wears on, the bakery fills with the sounds of chatter and laughter. Emma's regulars come and go, each one taking a bite of her magical bread. And as they do, Emma watches, mesmerized, as their memories unfold. She knows that her bread has the power to heal, to bring people together. And now, she has a new mystery to unravel – the mystery of the woman who came from her past.
